# Anti-AI Writing Rules (compact checklist)

Load this reference during any text-writing step. Full guide: `content-utilities/skills/humanizer/SKILL.md`

## Must avoid

1. **Significance inflation**: "pivotal moment", "evolving landscape", "testament to", "vital/crucial role"
2. **Promotional tone**: "vibrant", "groundbreaking", "nestled", "breathtaking", "boasts a"
3. **Superficial -ing phrases**: "highlighting...", "showcasing...", "underscoring...", "reflecting..."
4. **Vague attributions**: "Experts argue", "Industry reports suggest", "Observers note"
5. **AI vocabulary**: Additionally, delve, enhance, foster, garner, interplay, intricate, pivotal, showcase, tapestry, underscore, vibrant
6. **Copula avoidance**: "serves as" / "stands as" / "represents a" -> just use "is"
7. **Negative parallelisms**: "It's not just X; it's Y"
8. **Rule of three**: don't force ideas into groups of three
9. **Em dash overuse**: use commas or periods instead
10. **Boldface headers in lists**: "- **Category:** description..." -> write prose instead
11. **Filler phrases**: "In order to", "It is important to note that", "At this point in time"
12. **Sycophantic tone**: "Great question!", "Certainly!", "I hope this helps!"
13. **Generic conclusions**: "The future looks bright", "exciting times lie ahead"

## Must do

- **Vary sentence length**: short punchy, then longer flowing, then short again
- **Have opinions**: react to facts, don't just report them
- **Be specific**: "47% open rate" not "great results"
- **Use "is"/"are"/"has"** for simple statements
- **Acknowledge complexity**: "impressive but unsettling" beats "impressive"
- **First person when appropriate**: "I keep coming back to..." signals a real person
- **Read aloud test**: if it sounds stiff or assembled, rewrite it
