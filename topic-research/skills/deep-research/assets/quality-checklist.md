# Quality Checklist: Deep Research Article

Use this checklist before delivering Task 5 output.

---

## Content Completeness

- [ ] All 8 sections present (Hook, Technical, History, Landscape, Numbers, Trajectory, Risks, Takeaways)
- [ ] Word count: 3,000-8,000 words
- [ ] Sources section present with all citations

## Data & Citations

- [ ] Every major factual claim has a citation
- [ ] All URLs are clickable hyperlinks (not plain text)
- [ ] Tables have source lines
- [ ] Data matches Task 2 workbook (no discrepancies)
- [ ] Benchmark scores cite specific papers/leaderboards with dates
- [ ] Market size estimates cite specific reports with dates and methodology notes

## Visuals

- [ ] 15-25 visuals embedded
- [ ] All 4 mandatory visuals present:
  - [ ] Development timeline (chart_01)
  - [ ] Performance benchmarks (chart_05)
  - [ ] Adoption trend (chart_09)
  - [ ] Competitive positioning map (chart_14)
- [ ] All visuals have figure captions with source attribution
- [ ] Visuals placed near the text that discusses them
- [ ] Roughly 1 visual per 300-500 words

## Tables

- [ ] 8-15 data tables embedded
- [ ] Benchmark comparison table present
- [ ] Market size table present
- [ ] Adoption metrics table present
- [ ] Scenario summary table present
- [ ] All tables have source lines

## Editorial Quality

- [ ] Opening hook is specific and compelling (not a generic AI statement)
- [ ] Narrative hooks from Task 3 are woven throughout the article
- [ ] Article has a clear point of view, not just a survey
- [ ] Technical concepts are explained clearly for a smart non-expert
- [ ] No unsupported hype language
- [ ] Forward-looking section acknowledges uncertainty with confidence levels
- [ ] Risks section is honest and balanced

## Formatting

- [ ] Section headers are clear and descriptive
- [ ] Tables are properly formatted
- [ ] Figure captions follow format: `*Figure N: [Description]. Source: [Source].*`
- [ ] No orphaned references to data that aren't cited

## Final Check

- [ ] Read through entire article — does it flow?
- [ ] Would a first-time reader understand the topic after reading this?
- [ ] Would a content operator know exactly what article to write after reading this?
- [ ] Is the content recommendation (WRITE/MONITOR/SKIP) clearly stated and justified?
