# Quality Criteria Reference

Detailed scoring rubrics for each quality dimension in the quality-check skill.

## Fact Accuracy

**Pass**: All specific claims, statistics, and named references are verifiable and current. Citations present where needed.

**Flag**: 1-3 unverified claims that may be correct but lack citations. Or claims that are over 12 months old in a fast-moving field (e.g., AI capabilities).

**Fail**: Any demonstrably incorrect claim. Or 4+ unverified claims. Or statistics from clearly outdated sources cited as current.

### Common Accuracy Issues in AI Content
- Model capability claims that are version-specific but not versioned
- Benchmark scores without specifying benchmark version and date
- Company revenue or valuation figures without source date
- "As of [year]" claims in content from a prior year

## Logical Coherence

**Pass**: Clear thesis, each section advances the argument, evidence supports conclusions, transitions are smooth.

**Flag**: 1-2 logical gaps where a claim is made without supporting evidence, or a transition is abrupt. Overall argument still holds.

**Fail**: Conclusion contradicts evidence. Or major claim is made with no support. Or sections are disconnected from the stated purpose.

### Coherence Checklist
- [ ] Introduction states what the piece will cover
- [ ] Each section delivers what the intro implied
- [ ] Claims are followed by evidence or reasoning
- [ ] Opposing views are addressed if relevant
- [ ] Conclusion follows from the body, not from new information

## Readability

**Pass**: Avg sentence length ≤20 words, paragraphs ≤5 sentences, jargon defined, headings scan clearly.

**Flag**: Avg sentence length 20-30 words, or paragraphs 5-8 sentences, or technical terms used without definition for target audience.

**Fail**: Dense walls of text, avg sentence >30 words, no heading structure, assumes expertise the audience doesn't have.

### Reading Level Guidelines by Audience
| Audience | Target Level |
|----------|-------------|
| General public | 6th–8th grade (Flesch score 60-70) |
| Informed non-experts | 9th–12th grade (Flesch score 50-60) |
| Domain professionals | No strict limit; clarity still matters |

## Originality

**High**: Original research, novel synthesis, unique perspective, specific examples not found elsewhere.

**Medium**: Solid coverage of existing knowledge with a few original observations or a distinctive voice.

**Low**: Rehashes widely available information without adding analysis, perspective, or new examples. Reads like a summary of other summaries.

### Originality Signals
Positive: Primary research, first-hand examples, named sources with quotes, unique data, author's own experimentation
Negative: Generic examples ("imagine a user who wants to..."), unnamed sources ("experts say"), only cites other overview articles
